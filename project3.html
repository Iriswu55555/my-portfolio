Overview

This lesson plan integrates AI image recognition technology with STEAM interdisciplinary learning, guiding students from Grade 3 (elementary) to Grade 9 (junior high) to explore the National Palace Museum's bronze artifact collection from different dynasty. Through educational robots and interactive games, students experience the application of artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning, deepening their understanding of the artifacts’ visual features and cultural significance.

Tools

Tools Used: Microsoft Excel / Power BI / Tableau

Role

Workforce Analyst / Data Analyst

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the basic concepts of artificial intelligence and machine learning.
  • Operate AI platforms to build image classification and recognition models for museum artifacts.
  • Compare and analyze the visual features and cultural meanings of different bronze artifacts.
  • Develop computational thinking, logical reasoning, and interdisciplinary integration skills.
    • - **S (Science):** Bronze manufacturing, etching, and anti-corrosion methods - **T (Technology):** 3D printing, AI image recognition, programming - **E (Engineering):** Computational thinking (loops, conditionals) - **A (Art):** Visual design, color, and pattern recognition - **M (Mathematics):** Logical reasoning, classification algorithms

Outcomes

Post-launch surveys indicated a 30% increase in compliance knowledge and a 98% completion rate within the first quarter. The module was later adapted for contractor onboarding.
